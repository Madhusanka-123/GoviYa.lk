Short Duration Seeds

3 – 3.5 Months

These varieties mature quickly and are ideal for areas with water scarcity or for farmers who want to grow multiple crops per year. Best for Yala season in dry zones.

BG 300

  • Duration: 95–100 days
  • Yield: 4.5–5.0 t/ha
  • Best for: Dry zone, Yala season
  • Features: Drought tolerant, good grain quality
Recommended

BG 352

  • Duration: 100–105 days
  • Yield: 4.8–5.2 t/ha
  • Best for: Intermediate zone
  • Features: Disease resistant, high quality
Popular

AT 306

  • Duration: 90–95 days
  • Yield: 4.0–4.5 t/ha
  • Best for: Wet zone, Maha season
  • Features: Early maturing, good for organic farming

Medium Duration Seeds

4 – 4.5 Months

These varieties offer a balance between duration and yield, making them suitable for most farming conditions across Sri Lanka. Ideal for both Maha and Yala seasons.

BG 400

  • Duration: 120–130 days
  • Yield: 5.5–6.0 t/ha
  • Best for: All zones
  • Features: High yield, good tillering ability
Best Seller

AT 401

  • Duration: 125–135 days
  • Yield: 5.2–5.8 t/ha
  • Best for: Dry and intermediate zones
  • Features: Blast resistant, excellent grain quality
Premium

Bw 367

  • Duration: 115–125 days
  • Yield: 5.0–5.5 t/ha
  • Best for: Wet and intermediate zones
  • Features: Good for heavy soils, high milling quality

How to Choose the Right Seeds

Key factors to consider when selecting paddy seeds for your farm

1

Climate & Zone

Choose varieties suited to your region — wet zone, dry zone, or intermediate zone — for best performance.

2

Water Availability

Consider your irrigation access. Short duration seeds are best for areas with limited water supply.

3

Market Demand

Research which varieties command premium prices in your local market for better return on investment.

4

Disease Resistance

Select varieties with natural resistance to common diseases in your area to reduce crop losses.
